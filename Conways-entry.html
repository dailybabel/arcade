Conway's Game of Life is a zero-player cellular automaton where the entire universe unfolds from a single rule: each cell lives or dies based on how many neighbors it has. Drop a handful of cells and watch colonies explode, spaceships cruise across infinite space, and guns fire gliders forever — all from nothing but arithmetic applied repeatedly. It's the closest thing mathematics has to a creation myth.
